I am currently a full-time research assistant at IIT Madras under Dr. Mitesh Khapra and Dr. Pratyush Kumar. In the past I have also worked under Dr. Tanmoy Chakraborty at IIIT Delhi as a research intern.

Still being at the initial stages of learning, I am far from converging to a global minima of precise and concrete research interests. However, the idea of teaching machines to read, write and in turn understand our world - textually, visually, physically, and socially - stimulates me.

Since the past year, I have picked up a crucial problem of social biases in word representations as "The Endurance" to explore the vast field of natural language processing. Daring into the uncharted territories, I have managed to find many new insights and some solutions to the problem, presented as research papers and open source tools.

On my voyage, I have been amazed by the dominance of current language models, but have also witnessed its brittleness and a sheer lack of true understanding about the world. While the major problems may seem like a combination of a lack of social, and physical understanding leading to poor reasoning capacity. However, I believe that these can fundamentally be viewed as a problem of causality. Thus, I look forward to sail towards the ultimate objective of "Samantha", but now in a causally coherent way.

Projects

Fair Embedding Engine: A Library for Analyzing and Mitigating Gender Bias in Word Embeddings.
    FEE is an open-source library for the analysis of gender bias in word embeddings. It combines various state of the art techniques for quantifying, visualising and mitigating gender bias in word embeddings under a standard abstraction. Its design aids practitioners in fast track analysis of existing debiasing methods on their embedding models, and also allows rapid prototyping of new methods by evaluating their performance on a suite of standard metrics.
Seq2Seq
    A collection of three types of RNN or attention based sequence to sequence models with increasing complexity and performance. The models are trained and tested for machine translation.
    1) Seq2SeqExample - Multi-layer LSTM based Encoder-Decoder.
    2) Seq2SeqPR - Context Vector dependent GRU based Encoder-Decoder.
    3) Seq2SeqAttention - Attention dependent GRU based Encoder-Decoder.
