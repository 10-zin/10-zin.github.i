About Me

I am currently a full-time research assistant at IIT Madras under Dr. Mitesh Khapra and Dr. Pratyush Kumar. In the past I have also worked under Dr. Tanmoy Chakraborty at IIIT, Delhi as a research intern.

Still being at the initial stages of learning, I am far from converging to a global minima of precise and concrete research interests. However, the idea of teaching machines to read, write and in turn understand our world - textually, visually, physically, and socially - stimulates me.

Since the past year, I have picked up a crucial problem of social biases in word representations as "The Endurance" to explore the vast field of natural language processing. Daring into the uncharted territories, I have managed to find many new insights and some solutions to the problem, presented as research papers and open source tools.

On my voyage, I have been amazed by the dominance of current language models, but have also witnessed its brittleness and a sheer lack of true understanding about the world. Surveying various works I have realized the importance of making models more interpretable and robust. Additionally, moving in the direction of learning representations for language through grounding, and exploring linguistically informed inductive biases. Thus, I look forward to working on these sub-domains of NLP in the future.

Projects

Fair Embedding Engine: A Library for Analyzing and Mitigating Gender Bias in Word Embeddings.
  • FEE is an open-source library for the analysis of gender bias in word embeddings. It combines various state of the art techniques for quantifying, visualising and mitigating gender bias in word embeddings under a standard abstraction.
  • Its design aids practitioners in fast track analysis of existing debiasing methods on their embedding models, and also allows rapid prototyping of new methods by evaluating their performance on a suite of standard metrics.
Seq2Seq
  • A collection of three types of RNN or attention based sequence to sequence models with increasing complexity and performance. The models are trained and tested for machine translation.
    1) Seq2SeqExample - Multi-layer LSTM based Encoder-Decoder.
    2) Seq2SeqPR - Context Vector dependent GRU based Encoder-Decoder.
    3) Seq2SeqAttention - Attention dependent GRU based Encoder-Decoder.
A PyTorch implementation of the paper - " SYNTHESIZER: Rethinking Self-Attention in Transformer Models"
  • Transformers have become pervasive and they are powered by the fundamental scaled dot product attention mechanism. But are they necessary?
  • This paper majorly introduced two alternate forms of attentions:
    • Dense Attention + Its factorized version
    • Random Attention + Its factorized version
  • The authors of the paper have not yet released their code, but being shocked by the fact that thier Random Attention Model performed comparably with standard attention mechnainsm, I just couldn't resist.
  • Thus, this project. My results (tho at a lower scale due to lack of compute) convey the same too.
